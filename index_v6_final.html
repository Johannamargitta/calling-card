Hi, I’m Johanna, a graphic designer and second-year student at the Fachklasse Grafik in Lucerne.

I spent twelve days in the Passage Verdeau, observing and experiencing the space from within. During this time, I primarily engaged with the shopkeepers, whose daily presence shapes the passage. Through these encounters, I realized that the Passage Verdeau exists and comes to life because of them. It is a woven network of voices, routines, and personalities. These experiences and conversations formed the basis for the book Le tissage des voix.

Do you see clearly? I explored Emmenbrücke from a close and attentive perspective. Without searching for anything specific, I focused on observing the city and noticing its small, everyday details. By paying attention to what was seen and heard, I developed a nine-page booklet that reflects these subtle impressions.

A person trapped within a system. I translated a story I wrote into photography, capturing it in six images. Using the stories and photographs of my classmates, I then designed a book. Each story is given its own space within the publication.
